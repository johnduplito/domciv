Chapter I — The Land

In the beginning there was only the void and the hut. The first creature, Og, emerged blinking into a world full of elements to dismantle and resources to claim for the tribe.

The land was rich with paragraphs, headers, and nested structures. Each could be broken into raw material — hauled back to the settlement and turned into something greater.

Chapter II — The People

As the tribe grew, roles emerged naturally. Some became miners, chipping away at the page's structure. Others carried loads back to the settlement. A few stood watch, scanning the horizon for new elements to harvest.

  • Og — the first, stubborn, efficient
  • Ug — fast runner, small carry capacity
  • Zog — the builder, slow but precise
  • Bip — scout, ranges far from the hut
  • Mop — gatherer, prefers text nodes
  • Rar — warrior, guards the storehouse

Chapter III — The Structures

First came the Firepit, a simple gathering place for warmth and coordination. Then the Storehouse, to hold the growing surplus. The Workshop followed, allowing the tribe to refine raw materials into finer goods.

The Tower offered visibility across the whole page. And at last, the Temple — monument to something beyond mere survival, built from the bones of a hundred harvested elements.

Chapter IV — The Future

What lies beyond the temple? The tribe does not know. But they build, they harvest, they carry — and the civilisation grows, one demolished DOM element at a time.

More creatures mean faster harvesting. More buildings unlock new capabilities. The page will eventually be stripped bare — but from its ruins, something magnificent will rise.
